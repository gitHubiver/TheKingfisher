Another lovelorn poem about you

I was wrong.
I once believed that when love finally arrives,
you stop being the poet.
His giggles become stanzas with rhyme and meter.
The lines on his palm become traces of what was once battered and bruised
prose from nights before your paths crossed.
His collarbone draws metaphors for the moon,
the gleam in his eyes rivals the stars.
The thing is, my lover,
I didn't stop writing when I met you.
I wrote about you on sticky notes, coffee tables, banisters.
I scribbled your name on the cuffs of my jeans.
Every goodbye hug became the subject of my poetry and flash fiction.
When your lips met mine,
it felt like flipping through pages and chapters
of myself I had yet to explore.
I love you so much that my hands taught themselves to write lovelorn poems.
The ink in my veins craves your existence.

I now write this for you.

// written by r

To be feminine

Born with a larger structure,
ribs not much wider than her breast,
waist not much smaller than her hips,
and hands as big as men's.

Trouble picking out a dress,
slouching in case it is too bothering.
Smaller gestures take up little space,
then, keep the voice smaller than her frame.

Taller than males her age,
unpartnered on the promenade.
Indecision about wearing heels,
afraid like a mountain creature caving in.

Chest filled with questions
of how dainty others could be.
Refined in their cutest ways,
delicate in every way.

A staring contest with the mirror
asking what more she could do
to be as pretty and graceful as others do.
Maybe more ribbons in her hair would do…

// written by Christine Mae Altamerano

akinse

Ramdam ko pa rin ang pagkalas ng ‘yong kamay sa
aking mga daliri kahit isang taon na ang nakalipas
mula nang pakawalan natin ang isa’t isa.

Malinaw pa rin sa aking pandinig ang bawat kantang sumaliw sa indayog ng ating pagmamahalan habang
kumakain tayo sa restaurant—araw ng pag- ibig
at may special promo pa noon.Sabi mo, nahanap mo
ang kainang ito habang nagpaplano kayo
ng kaibigan kong si Rebecca ng magarbong pakulo upang tanungin ako ng pakikipag-isang-dibdib.

Kung kaya’t sinulit natin ang oras sa ilalim
ng buwan,ininom ang couple promo na beers,
at maging ang mga lasong dekorasyon ay hindi
nakaligtas—palihim kang kumuha ng
isa’t itinali sa aking buhok.

“Bagay ang kulay at hugis na ‘to sa ‘yong
mahabang buhok,” ani mo.

Ngunit pagpatak ng kinabukasa’y
kinalas mo ang laso’t sinabing hindi ka na
sigurado sa akin kagaya kung
gaano kabuo ang iyong loob kahapon.

Isang taon mula noon,
saka mo ito sinuot kay Rebecca—naniniwalang
mas bagay sa kan’ya.

// panulat ni norwen chris
// dibuho ni Junnie Goyada

KF YOUTH POINTS

OPINION | Silenced scream

It's harder to speak up when the laughter and mockery are deafening. As the world fights for equality, the remnants of past toxicity perpetuate. It was evident in the outdated and discriminatory gender norms imposed by society, which causes male victims of rape and sexual assault to suffer in silence. Their guts to bring their traumatic experience to light were deemed as a laughing stock. Moreover, the sad reality is that blame and degradation were thrown at the victim of abuse, rather than the justice and empathy that they deserve. Society discredited the fact that male victims also suffer and the sole culprit is the abuser. I believe, as we should, that sexual assault and the like are forms of abuse and one's chromosomal status should not dictate what kind of emotion victims are allowed to feel.

// written by Red Vauza

OPINION | On Merit

Voters tend to rate whether the credibility of electoral candidates weighs more than their compassion using socially constructed merit scales. During the 10-day campaign period from October 19 to 28, endorsement of credentials and promises of good governance echoed in the alleys and knocked on the front doors of local communities. As the Barangay and Sangguniang Kabataan Elections (BSKE) return after its long delay of commencement, more than 1.41 million aspiring village and youth council leaders will be scrutinized this year, a huge increment from the one million number of filed certificates of candidacy (COC) in 2018, according to the Commission on Elections (Comelec).


// written by Sophia Mateo



KF YOUTH POINTS

OPINION | Educational Dystopia

Academic achievements matter in a society where report cards are considered keys to various possibilities. People often say that academic grades do not define students for they are just numbers. Therefore, a poor or excellent performance reflected in the historical academic record supposedly does not determine our future. However, this is not the case for underprivileged students who are incapable of making ends meet. Getting a failing grade is never an option if they want to be admitted to college and eventually obtain scholarship grants. At present, a lot of educational institutions prioritize good academic performers as grades are considered an important factor in college admissions.



// written by Angielene
